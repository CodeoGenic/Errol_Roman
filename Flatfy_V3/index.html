Profile

Errol Roman is currently employed as a cognitive behavioural psychotherapist () in the Long-Term Psychology Team in North East London Foundation (NHS) Trust. As a cognitive behavioural psychotherapist he works in the Integrative Diabetes Service where he is responsible for the assessment and delivery of psychological care to clients with diabetes. Errol Roman is primarily a classical practitioner however has had extensive training and experience in Metacognitive Therapy (MCT), Compassion Focussed Therapy (CFT) Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT) and Strengths-based Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (SB-CBTCBTCBT) and so draws on his experience of these theoretical frameworks in the development of an understanding of his clients and consequently any difficulties that they may be experiencing. He has also undertaken extensive literature review on resilience that enables him to use SB-CBT in his treatment of clients with diabetes. In addition to his training as a cognitive behavioural psychotherapist Errol Roman has Diploma in Alcohol Counselling and Consultation and as such has been trained in the additive behaviours and went on to develop particular interest and competencies in motivational interviewing as a technique in the treatment of clients who are dependent on alcohol. He has extensive training and experience in alcohol and drug dependence and previously worked as Team Manager for drugs and alcohol services in addition to his managerial experiences in older people and adult mental health services. Errol Roman started his professional career as a primary school teacher after his training at Church Teacher’s College, Mandeville, phobias, health anxiety, post-traumatic stress disorder, generalised anxiety disorder, obsessional compulsive disorder) depression, low self-esteem, grief and bereavement, drugs and alcohol dependence, building resilience, and he also trained to and works effectively with couples. Errol Roman is an accomplished cognitive behavioural psychotherapist and following his training he has been accredited by the British Association for Behavioural and Cognitive Psychotherapies (BABCP) and maintains his professional practice in accordance with their ethical and professional standards of being non-judgemental and confidential. He also maintains his registration with the Nursing and Midwifery Council (NMC) where, in addition to the BABCP he is accountable for his professional practice.

Teacher/Training

Errol Roman was trained as a primary school teacher at Church Teacher’s College, Mandeville Jamaica and worked in primary education before migrating to the United Kingdom (UK). He is a very skilful trainer in motivational interviewing, skills in cognitive behavioural psychotherapies and an array of family life education skills such as communication and problem solving.

Other Interest

Errol Roman is a member of the Seventh-day Adventist Church where he undertakes most of his practices in family life education with his wife Sonia Roman who also holds a certificate in family life education (CFLE). Together they ran family life education training on marriage, parenting, mental health, being single, and developed the Older People Social Club which offers information and interaction for older people in the local community. He is fascinated by theology and draws inspiration from the twelve very ordinary uneducated men from the lower social class that Jesus Christ used to transform the history to the world.
