Errol Roman

Cognitive Behavioural Psychotherapist

Profile

Errol Roman is currently employed as a cognitive behavioural psychotherapist (CBT) in the Long-Term Condition Psychology Team in North East London Foundation (NHS) Trust. As a cognitive behavioural psychotherapist he works in the Integrative Diabetes Service where he is responsible for the assessment, collaborative care planning and delivery of psychological care to clients with diabetes. Errol Roman was trained in Metacognitive Therapy (MCT) however in practice he is primarily a classical CBT practitioner with sound experience in Compassion Focussed Therapy (CFT) Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT), and Strengths-based Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (SB-CBT) and so draws on his experience of these theoretical frameworks in the development of an understanding of his clients and consequently any difficulties that they may be experiencing. He has also undertaken extensive research on resilience and this enables him to use SB-CBT to reinforce clientâ€™s resilience. In addition to his training as a cognitive behavioural psychotherapist Errol Roman has Diploma in Alcohol Counselling and Consultation and as such has been trained in the additive behaviours and went on to develop particular interest and competencies in motivational interviewing as a technique in the treatment of clients who are dependent on alcohol. He has extensive training and experience in alcohol and drug dependence and previously worked as Team Manager for drugs and alcohol services in addition to his managerial experiences in older people and adult mental health services.

Errol Roman started his professional career as a primary school teacher after his training at Church Teacherâ€™s College, Mandeville, Jamaica. Following his internship at Beulah Primary School, Clarendon, he worked in Jubilee Town (where he grew up and went to school) and St. Johnâ€™s Primary Schools, St Catherine before migrating to the United Kingdom where he has been trained as: a psychiatric nurse, a counsellor, a psychotherapist and a family life educator. In addition to his expertise in clinical health psychology in the long-term condition (diabetes) team Errol Roman is competent in the treatment of the anxiety disorders (social anxiety, panic disorder, panic attacks, phobias, health anxiety, post-traumatic stress disorder, generalised anxiety disorder (worry), obsessional compulsive disorder) depression, low self-esteem, grief and bereavement, drugs and alcohol dependence, and building resilience . As a family life educator he has been trained in couple counselling and works effectively with couples in strengthening their relationship and or come to some mutual understanding.

Errol Roman is evidently an accomplished cognitive behavioural psychotherapist and following his training he has been accredited by the British Association for Behavioural and Cognitive Psychotherapies (BABCP) and maintains his professional practice in accordance with their ethical and professional standards of being non-judgemental, confidential, supervised practice and continuous professional development. He also maintains his registration with the Nursing and Midwifery Council (NMC) where, in addition to the BABCP he is held accountable for his professional practice
